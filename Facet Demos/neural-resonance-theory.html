02 / The Proposed Mechanism

How It Could Work

1

CALIBRATION

Conversation and shared stimuli align internal representations. Like tuning a crystal radio to a specific frequency.

2

EMISSION

Neural firing creates weak EM fields (proven via EEG). Synchronous firing amplifies the signal through coherent summation.

3

OPENING

Neurons near firing threshold create periodic "windows" of sensitivity. Reception may be phase-dependent—gates that open at specific oscillatory moments.

4

RESONANCE

Compatible frequencies lock. Similar architectures decode similar patterns. Not transmitting thoughts—maintaining temporal coordination.

03 / Proposed Experiments

Testing the Hypothesis

EXPERIMENT 01

Threshold Window Test

Apply weak EM pulses at brain-emission intensities, timed to different phases of the subject's alpha rhythm.

Prediction: Neural response differs based on oscillatory phase—proving "openings" exist.

EXPERIMENT 02

Frequency-Matched Coupling

Measure subject's dominant alpha frequency. Apply external EM at matching vs. mismatched frequencies.

Prediction: Stronger response when external signal matches individual's intrinsic rhythm.

EXPERIMENT 03

Skull Piezoelectric Test

Test whether skull bone transduces between EM and mechanical vibration at neural frequencies.

Prediction: Bidirectional conversion provides a previously unknown reception mechanism.

EXPERIMENT 04

Co-Listening Test

Calibrate two similar-brain subjects, isolate them, record EEG. Measure correlation above chance.

Prediction: Calibrated similar pairs show correlation; uncalibrated/dissimilar pairs show none.

EXPERIMENT 05

Controlled EM Link

Isolated rooms with controllable EM pathway. Compare synchronization with link open vs. closed.

Prediction: Allowing brain-frequency EM to pass increases measurable synchronization.

06 / Synthesis

What We're Testing

THE THEORY

  • Brains emit weak EM fields
  • Similar architectures have compatible frequencies
  • Calibration aligns oscillatory parameters
  • Phase-dependent "openings" allow reception
  • Coupling maintains temporal sync, not content

IF TRUE

  • New sensory/communication channel discovered
  • Explains "same wavelength" phenomenon
  • Predicts stronger effect for similar pairs
  • Requires prior calibration interaction
  • Opens questions about distributed cognition

IF FALSE

  • Brain EM too weak for inter-brain coupling
  • All sync explained by known channels
  • Similarity causes parallel, not coupled, outputs
  • Subjective experience remains metaphorical
  • Focus shifts to enhancing known mechanisms

THE KEY INSIGHT

The model doesn't require telepathy or paranormal claims. It asks a narrower question: Can brains receive at the frequencies they emit? If yes, the rest follows from physics. If no, the theory fails cleanly. Either outcome advances understanding.
