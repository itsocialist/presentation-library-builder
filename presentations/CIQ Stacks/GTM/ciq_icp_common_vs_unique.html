03 / THE SPLIT

Common vs. Unique Characteristics

What brings customers together, what sets them apart

✓ COMMON TRAITS
  • Firmographic: 500-10K employees, $500M-$10B revenue
  • Infrastructure: 100+ Linux nodes, established IT teams
  • Budget Authority: $50K-$2M+ annual infrastructure spend
  • Geography: Global with US/EMEA focus
  • Migration Driver: Moving from RHEL/CentOS/Legacy
  • Decision Process: 3-9 month cycles, 5-7 stakeholders
  • Personas: CIO, IT Director, Infrastructure Lead
  • Buying Trigger: Cost reduction + vendor independence
⚡ UNIQUE TRAITS
  • RLC-H: STIG/CMMC compliance mandatory, CISO buyer
  • RLC-AI: 50+ GPUs, AI/ML infrastructure lead buyer
  • Fuzzball: Multi-cluster environment, hybrid cloud strategy
  • Warewulf: Bare metal HPC, 50-10K nodes, research computing
  • Ascender: Ansible expertise, no 100-node minimum need
  • Apptainer: HPC container workloads, scientific computing

05 / THE VERTICALS

Industry Overlap Patterns

Where products naturally bundle

HIGH OVERLAP
  • Defense/Federal: RLC-H + Warewulf + Fuzzball (compliance + HPC cluster)
  • Research/Academic: Warewulf + Fuzzball + RLC-AI (HPC + AI/ML)
  • Life Sciences: RLC-AI + Fuzzball (AI drug discovery + HPC simulation)
  • Financial Services: RLC-H + Ascender + RLC-AI (security + automation + quant AI)
MEDIUM OVERLAP
  • Healthcare: RLC-H + RLC-AI (HIPAA + medical AI)
  • Technology/SaaS: RLC-AI + Ascender (AI infrastructure + DevOps)
  • Manufacturing: RLC-H + Warewulf (security + simulation)
  • Aerospace: RLC-H + Fuzzball (security + complex HPC)

Bundle Opportunity

60% of non-RLC deals are bundled — products with high ICP overlap naturally sell together. Defense/Federal and Research/Academic show strongest multi-product fit.
